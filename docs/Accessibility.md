# Accessibility Statement - The Outliers Studio

*Last Updated: July 20, 2026*

**The Outliers Studio** is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continuously improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards to make our premium streetwear e-commerce platform inclusive.

---

## 1. Conformance Status
We strive to align our Website (`[WEBSITE_URL]`) with the **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA** standards. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with sensory, cognitive, and mobility disabilities.

---

## 2. Key Accessibility Features Implemented
To ensure a highly accessible shopping experience, we design our Next.js and Tailwind-based interface with the following configurations:
* **Semantic HTML**: We use standard HTML5 semantic elements (`<header>`, `<main>`, `<nav>`, `<footer>`, `<section>`, etc.) to ensure screen readers can navigate our page layouts logically.
* **Keyboard Navigation**: The site's primary user flows—including product browsing, size selection, adding to cart, and checking out—are fully operable using keyboard inputs (Tab, Enter, Space, Arrow keys).
* **Color Contrast**: We maintain text-to-background color contrast ratios meeting WCAG AA requirements to ensure readability, especially on dark-mode bento layouts.
* **Image Alternative Text**: We provide descriptive `alt` tags for all product images, campaign lookbooks, and branding graphic assets.
* **ARIA Attributes**: Accessible Rich Internet Applications (ARIA) attributes are used where necessary to define dynamic components (like sliding cart drawers, size selectors, and modal popups).

---

## 3. Technical Specifications
Accessibility of our Website relies on the following technologies to work with the particular combination of web browser and any assistive technologies or plugins installed on your computer:
* HTML5
* CSS3 / Tailwind Utility Tokens
* TypeScript / JavaScript
* WAI-ARIA

---

## 4. Known Limitations & Areas of Improvement
While we aim for full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, some sections may have limitations:
* **Third-Party Integrations**: Third-party features, such as the embedded Razorpay checkout modal or external courier tracking widgets, are controlled by their respective developers and may not fully meet our internal accessibility benchmarks.
* **Interactive Sizing Tools**: Some interactive 3D models or visual size estimators may require mouse inputs. We provide static tabular alternatives for all sizing details as a fallback.
